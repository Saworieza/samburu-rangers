About Us

About SLCRSG

Samburu Landscapes Community Rangers Support Group (SLCRSG) is a community-based organization (CBO) registered under the relevant laws of Kenya and operating within the Samburu landscapes and the larger Northern Kenya conservation area.

SLCRSG was founded by community members who run or are members of various community conservancies existing within the Samburu landscapes. Under the leadership of the Chairman, Mr Michael Lenaimado, the organization was formally registered as CBO with its head office in Wamba Township, Samburu County in Kenya, and a coordination office in Rongai, Kajiado County.

Our Vision & Mission

VISION To be the facilitator of community Wildlife Rangers in Samburu Landscapes for effective and responsible discharge of their conservation duties.

MISSION To help Samburu Landscapes rangers in providing networks, representation and ensuring they have the necessary skills, equipment, and are well motivated to perform their duties

Area Coverage

At present there are more than 160 conservancies in Kenya that cover approximately 10% of the country's terrestrial land surface area and draw memberships from a total of 707, 460 local households (world bank Report 2020).
Most of the conservancies were established on lands adjacent to statutory protected areas in arid and semi-arid ecosystems where poverty levels are relatively high. Samburu Landscapes Conservation area is a host of ten such Conservancies, most of which were established recently starting in the 2000s

These community conservancies include mainly those within Samburu County, and some from the neighboring counties like Isiolo and Marsabit.

The more than 10 community wildlife conservancies within the Samburu Landscapes area DO NOT comprise the Samburu National Reserve, Buffalo springs National Reserve and Shaba national Reserve which have Direct Govenment Support
